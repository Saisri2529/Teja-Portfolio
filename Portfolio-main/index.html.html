Roadside Vegetation Detection through Dashcam

Deep Learning for Roadside Vegetation Detection

In the realm of autonomous driving technology, accurately identifying roadside vegetation presents a significant challenge due to its diverse characteristics. This project introduces a deep learning-based approach utilizing a fine-tuned ResNet-50 model to detect and segment roadside vegetation from dashcam images. Leveraging transfer learning on the MIT DriveSeg Dataset, our method showcases robust performance metrics, including an accuracy of 83.76%, precision of 84.87%, and recall of 91.54%. By enhancing the model's ability to perceive vegetation in dynamic driving scenes, this research contributes to advancing the safety and efficiency of autonomous driving systems.Github

TV News Channel Commercial Detection

TV News Channel Commercial Detection Project Report

The TV News Channel Commercial Detection project aims to classify video clips as commercial or non-commercial using machine learning techniques. The dataset, sourced from the UCI machine learning repository, comprises 150 hours of video recordings with features like Motion distribution and Edge change ratio extracted using complex algorithms. Preprocessing involved expanding the dense matrix into a sparse CSV format for analysis. Various models including SVM, Random Forest, and Deep Learning were evaluated, with SVM demonstrating the highest accuracy. Feature selection techniques like Lasso and bi-directional elimination reduced the feature set significantly while maintaining accuracy. Clustering using K-means identified optimal clusters, and visualization using PCA and Kernel PCA illustrated data separation. Ensemble modeling improved accuracy to 85%. The project concludes with insights on model performance and recommendations for further exploration.Github
